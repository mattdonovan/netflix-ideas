# Netflix design careers — interview reel (~2018)

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1N2TClkX5rU
**Type:** Short recruitment/culture video, multiple speakers
**Era marker (per user):** 8 years old at fetch — places it around 2018

> ⚠️ **Context source, not Hawkins-specific.** Hawkins is never mentioned. The value of this clip is establishing what Netflix Design's *self-presentation* was 3 years before the Hawkins-public-talks era. Useful for the timeline + understanding the culture into which Hawkins was being built.

## Key claims

- **"When did you know joining Netflix was the right decision?"** — Speaker recalls sitting in a meeting with a PM on the left and a business-development partner on the right, talking business strategy. "This is a conversation I've never been able to have in my career and that's to me is when I knew okay this is the right decision." Implication: designers are seated at strategy tables, not handed mocks downstream.
- **Data-powered creativity** — "A lot of our design solutions are powered by data and research and findings that we may have had and I feel like that really does empower our creativity."
- **Breadth of asset-level vs. system-level thinking** — "Designers are given so much breadth to really come up with different ways that they want to not only design individual assets but think about the entire asset suite as a whole." (Implies a culture already thinking in systems before Hawkins was public.)
- **Producers + designers as paired roles** — "We have producers that help guide and help disseminate information and we have designers who actually create those assets and come up with those amazing innovative canvases."
- **Constant relaunch cadence** — "We're relaunching our interfaces you know constantly every year in little ways and large ways."
- **Self-directed contribution model** — "When you join Netflix you have the ability to impact any part of the product that you feel passionate about... you really get to set out to what your heart feels passionate about rather than just being forced down a path."
- **Hiring beyond visual + interaction designers** — "We don't just have visual designers and interaction designers on Netflix. We have ex film directors, we have people that have designed huge art exhibitions. We like to bring in people who can bring a different perspective in how entertainment can fit your life... that could be music, that could be games, sports — however people feel like they can relax and how they can get deeper engagement out of entertainment."

## Threads worth pulling

- The "designer-as-strategic-partner" framing here pre-dates the Schema 2021 talk's "guidance over governance" frame by ~3 years. Same DNA, different vocabulary.
- "Self-directed contribution" is the same shape as the bottom-up engineering decision-making Elizabeth Stone describes in 2025 and the ex-employee describes in 2023. **Same cultural value, observed from three vantage points across 7 years.**

## Full transcript

> At what point did you know that joining the Netflix team was the right decision?
>
> That's a good question. I was sitting in a meeting and like to my left I had a PM, to the right I had our business development partner, and we're talking about the business strategy and I kind of took a step back from that moment because this is a conversation I've never been able to have in my career and that's to me is like when I knew okay this is the right decision.
>
> A lot of our design solutions are powered by data and research and findings that we may have had and I feel like that really does empower our creativity.
>
> Designers are given so much breadth to really come up with different ways that they want to not only design individual assets but think about the entire asset suite as a whole. Designers have kind of a unique opportunity to think way outside the box. They have the opportunity to really think how can I make this product overall better.
>
> We have producers that help guide and help disseminate information and we have designers who actually create those assets and come up with those amazing innovative canvases. I mean we're relaunching our interfaces you know constantly every year in little ways and large ways and that couldn't be done without these amazing designers.
>
> When you join Netflix you have the ability to impact any part of the product that you feel passionate about. It depends where I feel like my skill sets can be the most valuable in a project in somebody else's project. You really get to set out to what your heart feels passionate about rather than just being forced down a path.
>
> We don't just have visual designers and interaction designers on Netflix. We have ex film directors, we have people that have designed huge art exhibitions. We like to bring in people who can bring a different perspective in how entertainment can fit your life and that's not necessarily from a TV and film perspective. That could be music, that could be games, sports — however people feel like they can relax and how they can get deeper engagement out of entertainment is somebody that we're [looking for / interested in].
