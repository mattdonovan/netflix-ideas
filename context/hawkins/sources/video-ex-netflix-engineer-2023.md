# Ex-Netflix engineer — what made Netflix different (~2023)

## Key claims

- **Top-of-personal-market compensation** — "They'll pay you the most money that you can get in the market. So if you want to get more money you can go interview at Google or another company, take the offer back to Netflix, and Netflix will beat that offer." (Matches Elizabeth Stone's 2025 "personal top of market" phrasing exactly.)
- **High expectations come with the money** — "You're an A player, you're an engineering rockstar, so we need to see that rockstar value from you."
- **Bottom-up decision making at Netflix vs. top-down at Facebook/Airbnb** — "At Facebook and Airbnb a lot of the decision making happens like top down where the managers say you should work on these projects. Whereas when it's bottom up the engineers decide what projects to work on." (Echoes Stone's "innovation is really driven from within the teams rather than top down" 2 years later.)
- **The "convince another team" mechanic** — "When you're trying to work on a more complex project that needs to have multiple teams involved, then you have to go and convince each of the teams individually as opposed to having a manager tell them 'you need to work with Zach on this project.' Zach has to go and convince them that this project is valuable enough to spend their time on."
- **Side effect: built him into a strong communicator** — "I really liked it because I think it made me a way better communicator... that whole process has just made me more persuasive and more engaging and better at storytelling."

## Full transcript

> Working at Netflix was so different from the other big tech jobs that I worked. So I've worked in big tech for like about the last seven years. I've worked at Airbnb, Facebook and Netflix. And Netflix was the one that was the most different.
>
> I'll just give a couple examples. One — Netflix the way compensation worked when I worked at Netflix was they paid you top of personal market which essentially means they'll pay you the most money that you can get in the market. So if you want to get more money you can go maybe interview at Google or interview at another company and then they'll give you an offer. You can take it back to Netflix and then Netflix will beat that offer and then they will give you a raise. And that's a really amazing thing that Netflix could do. They just paid every employee so much money. It was like insane how much money they would pay each employee.
>
> That was something I found very exciting about working there. But on the flip side, that came with a lot of expectations. They expect you to deliver on value. You're an A player, you're an engineering rockstar, so we need to see that rockstar value from you. And so that part of working at Netflix was definitely one of the parts that I found to be kind of challenging — that money came with some pretty heavy expectations.
>
> And the other part about it was that Netflix is more bottom up. So the decision making really does happen more from individual engineers, whereas at Facebook and Airbnb a lot of the decision making happens top down where the managers like you should work on these projects. Whereas when it's bottom up the engineers decide what projects to work on, and that can be really good but also kind of tricky. It's good because then the engineers are closest to the problem so they probably have a better idea of what to work on. But it's tricky because when you're trying to work on a more complex project that needs to have multiple teams involved, then you have to go and convince each of the teams individually — as opposed to having a manager tell them 'you need to work with Zach on this project.' No, Zach has to go and convince them that this project is valuable enough to spend their time on.
>
> And that part of working at Netflix was tricky. I really liked it because I think it made me a way better communicator and it was a big part of how I've been able to get good at content. That whole process has just made me more persuasive and more engaging and better at storytelling. I really think that working at Netflix really trained me at being good at those things. But yeah, it's definitely a very unique experience. I highly recommend working at Netflix if you ever want to really up-level your career and take your career to the next level. It's really intense but it's really worth it as well.
